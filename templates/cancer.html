What is Cancer?

Cancer is a group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells in the body. These cells can form tumors, invade nearby tissues, and spread to other parts of the body. It can affect virtually any organ and is a leading cause of death worldwide. Early diagnosis and appropriate treatment significantly improve survival rates.

How Does Cancer Develop?

Cancer develops when normal cells undergo genetic changes (mutations) that cause them to grow uncontrollably. Contributing factors include:

  • Genetics: Inherited gene mutations can predispose individuals to certain cancers.
  • Environmental Factors: Exposure to carcinogens like tobacco, radiation, and harmful chemicals.
  • Unhealthy Lifestyle: Poor diet, lack of exercise, smoking, and alcohol consumption.
  • Chronic Inflammation: Long-term inflammation can increase the risk of DNA damage.
  • Age: The likelihood of developing cancer increases as people age.

How Can Cancer Be Prevented?

While not all cancers are preventable, many can be avoided or delayed by adopting a healthy lifestyle and minimizing risk factors:

  • Avoid Smoking: Tobacco use is one of the leading causes of cancer. Quitting smoking can dramatically reduce risk.
  • Maintain a Healthy Diet: Eat a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid processed and red meats.
  • Stay Active: Regular exercise helps maintain a healthy weight and reduces cancer risk.
  • Limit Alcohol Consumption: Alcohol is a known risk factor for several types of cancer.
  • Protect Your Skin: Use sunscreen and avoid excessive sun exposure to reduce the risk of skin cancer.
  • Get Vaccinated: Vaccines like HPV and Hepatitis B protect against infections linked to certain cancers.
  • Regular Screenings: Routine checkups and screenings help detect cancer early, when treatment is most effective.

Why Early Detection Matters

Early detection of cancer increases the chances of successful treatment and can save lives. Regular screenings and prediction systems like this one can identify early warning signs before symptoms appear, enabling prompt medical intervention.
